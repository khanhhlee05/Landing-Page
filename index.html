And this is
my journey

Hailing from Vietnam, Khanh Le is a globally-minded student currently pursuing a major in Computer Engineering at Villanova University. His educational journey spans multiple countries, including the majority of his early education in Vietnam and his stepping stone at the Eastern Mediterranean International School (EMIS) in Israel, where he developed a deep appreciation for diverse cultures and global perspectives. Khanh is driven by a passion for using technology as a force for good. He believes in the power of technological innovation to create positive change and is keenly interested in exploring the vast potential of technology in shaping a better world.

A quick walkthrough of my life

2005 - 2021

My early education
in Vietnam

Born and raised in Vietnam, I'm deeply grateful for the diverse environment that created who I am. Here, I am a basketball player, a boy scout instilled, and a boy with a spirit of adventure, teamwork, and resilience; these traits of me has made active, talkative, and always eager to explore new opportunities.

2021 - 2023

A transformative step
in Israel

Studying at EMIS in Israel was a life-changing experience. I co-led a team in developing innovative robots, competed nationally, and designed a device to convert air into water. It was here that I found my passion for technology and a vision to use it for social good, all while embracing diverse cultures and global perspectives.

2023 - Present

On my journey to sharpen myself at Villanova

Coming to the Villanova gives me the capabilities to accomplish my goals. Having access to Villanova Computer Engineering's network and resources allows me to enrich my tech knowledge deeply. Here, I also participate in organizations to promote diversity, such as VISO (International Students) and SASE (Asians Engineers & Scientists,...); most importantly, I'm still that active, energetic boy from Vietnam by participating in intramural basketball, futsal, soccer, and volleyball.
